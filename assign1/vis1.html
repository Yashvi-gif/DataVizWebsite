Percentage contribution of different sources of energy to different states and territories in Australia, 2019-2020

Australian Energy Statistics

the authoritative and official source of energy statistics for Australia

Graph Analysis

The visualisation is a Stacked Bar Chart which displays the Percentage contribution of different sources of energy to different states and territories in Australia, 2019-2020. On the x-axis we have the Percentage of energy contribution and on the y-axis we have the different states and territories of Australia. The state attribute is categorical since they are different categories while the Percentage attribute is ratio as percent can be 0 or any other number. The channel type is Color Hue and the mark type is Area. We have used this colour-scheme to cater to the large audience in consideration with our color-blind audience. Towards the right is the legend which depicts which color stands for which energy. We can observe that the consumption of coal is highest in NSW and nearly 0 in NT. There is a significant difference between the contribution of Gas in NT and other states. Moreover, Oil and gas are two major sources of energy in NT. TAS is the only state where the contribution of Renewable source of energy is the largest. Other states and territories have minimal contribution of Renewable energy sources. This indicates that TAS is moving towards sustainability at a higher rate that the other regions. ON the contrary, WA has as low as 2.3% of renewable energy contribution.
